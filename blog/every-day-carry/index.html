Every Day Carry and Equipment

published on 21 Feb 2023

I get asked for recommendations often regarding technology, clothing, boots, watches, etc and while these are topics I could talk about for hours on end, I simply don’t have the time to do so sometimes. As such I thought it would be useful to create an easy to navigate index of what I have, use and recommend that I can share with those interested.

As with the library this list is likely incomplete and poorly formatted. It will get better over time (hopefully) unless I forget (perhaps).

Equipment:

This is my currently equipped items.

Luggage

  • Backpack

    • Mil-Tec Molle Tactical Assault Pack
      • A high quality reproduction / knock-off of some military design backpack. It is waterproof enough for my needs and can carry a significant amount of cargo.
      • The listings for these online will say it is 20l or 25l I’m not sure which is accurate or if some are only listing the main compartment. Regardless this bag is excellent for my needs. I bought a second one for this reason.
      • I paid ¬£35 for each of them, I would recommend them at this price. However if you can get something with equal features without a tactical / military look to it that may be the more practical option.

  • Gym Bag

    • Adidas Essentials Linear Duffel Bag Medium
      • This 40 liter duffel bag is huge, it can hold all of my gym equipment and then some and it weighs basically nothing.
      • I’m not sure about its durability but I have had no issues with it so far. It has held up better than more expensive bags I have used in the past.
      • I paid ¬£15 for it. I’d recommend it at this price.

Computers

Listed in order of most frequent use:

  1. Macbook Air M1

    • First generation base model Macbook Air running Apple silicon.
    • Ideal University computer due to battery life and compatibility with required software.
    • See my current and recommend accessories.

  2. Custom Desktop PC

    • Specs:
      • R5 2600 CPU
      • RX 580
      • 32GB DDR4 RAM
    • Artix Linux System
    • Gaming capable, not that I really bother these days.

  3. ThinkPad T440p

    • Portable Linux computer. Fairly well upgraded.
    • For when I need penguin mode on the go. Which I haven’t recently hence it’s location on the list.

  4. ThinkPad X230

    • Backup for the T440p

Portable Electronics

  • Headphones

    • Sennheiser HD450SE Wireless Headphones
      • Perfect headphones for traveling. Not too powerful, they don’t need to be. Comfortable enough, may need slackened (at your own risk) depending on your head shape / size. I paid ¬£70 for them. I would recommend them at this price.

  • Mobile Phone

    • Google Pixel 7
      • Charcoal Black model of course.

Peripherals

  • Macbook Dock

    • ABIWAZY USB C Docking Station
      • Generic Thunderbolt docking station for laptops that support it.
      • Can convert my Macbook to a desktop at home, small enough to travel with in case I need an Ethernet connection or HDMI output.
      • You can likely find a similar or better solution for the price. I paid ¬£52 and don’t regret the purchase.

  • USB-A to USB-C Adapters

    • 2x Google Pixel OEM USB-C adapters
      • I use these for the Google Pixel, they work in the Macbook but are a bit bulky for it in my use case.
    • 2x Syntech USB-A to USB-C adapters
      • Sleeker and less bulky option for use with the Macbook, can’t use both at the same time due to size. I carry two anyway.
  • Portable Storage
    • Crucial 500GB MX500 SATA SSD
      • With USB enclosure.

Stationary

  • A4 Refill Pad
    • Self-explanatory. I need to take notes and do classwork. These are cheap and seemingly infinitely available.
  • 4 Colour Pens
    • A pen enclosure with 4 different coloured pens inside. Incredibly useful for language study.
    • Provides red (corrections), black (writing), blue (translation) and green (inline note taking).
  • Generic branded Post-Its
    • For page marking and note taking in my textbooks.
    • I am aware that these deteriorate books over time, I am looking for a better alternative.

Footwear

  • Bundeswehr Fallschrimjager Boots Mk4

    • Finest combat boots ever made. Perfectly comfortable, keeps my feet warm and dry, slip on and off no issue when needed.
    • Resoled with Vibram soles.

  • Converse Chuck Taylor All Star Hi Top

    • Perfect flat shoe for the gym.
    • Cheap, durable, flat, available in large sizes.

Watch

  • Casio G-Shock GA-110
    • Indestructible digital timepiece.
