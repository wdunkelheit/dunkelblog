Minute Mead

published on 27 Feb 2023

This is a recipe for making “minute” mead. Meaning that it can be made quickly and cheaply. This is not a traditional mead recipe but it is a cheaper and easier alternative that is great for beginners to try out before buying expensive ingredients to try for the first time.

Ingredients

  • 8 litres of Water
  • 4 Lemons
  • 500g Brown sugar
  • 500g honey
  • Yeast
  • Raisins (Optional) (Recommended)

Directions

  1. Activate your yeast in warm water.
  2. Wash and zest and juice lemons.
  3. Add lemon zest, sugar and honey to a 10 litre pot or bucket.
  4. Boil 4 litres of water and add to the sugar mixture.
  5. Mix the sugar mixture and boiling water until it is one fluid.
  6. Add the remainder of the water.
  7. Add the lemon juice.
  8. Continue mixing and wait for the fluid to reach blood temperature.
  9. Add yeast. Observe for bubbles. Once bubbling starts, add a lid to the pot and cover with a towel or old t-shirt.
  10. Wait 24 hours.
  11. You can now begin bottling the mead.
  12. Once bottled, add some raisins to the fluid with one teaspoon of sugar per litre.
  13. Store in your fridge for 3-5 days, release gas from the bottles in the morning and at night.

Notes

  1. This recipe is proportional, if you want a smaller batch, adjust each ingredient by the same fraction.
  2. For this recipe I use two 5 litre pots instead of a single pot or bucket.
  3. The raisins are used for measuring the state of the mead. The more of them that float the closer it is to being done.
    • Note that it is possible that not all of them will float by the time the mead is finished.
  4. Try experimenting with adding fruits or heather.
  5. Adjust sugar levels for sweetness / sourness, etc.
