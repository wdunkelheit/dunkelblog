Cranmeode - Cranberry Mead

published on 20 Mar 2023

This is a recipe for making Cranmeode or cranberry mead if you prefer. It has a sweet taste and fizzy finish.

Ingredients

  • 4 litres of Water
  • 1kg honey ( You can replace 500g of honey with brown sugar )
  • 300g Cranberries
  • Yeast
  • Raisins (Optional) (Recommended)

Directions

  1. Activate your yeast in warm water.
  2. Add honey to a 5 litre pot or bucket.
  3. Boil 4 litres of water and add to the pot.
  4. Mix the honey and boiling water until it is one fluid.
  5. Add the cranberries.
  6. Allow to simmer for about 20 minutes.
  7. Add water as needed / wanted.
  8. Set the pot aside and allow to cool.
  9. Once the mixture is blood temperature or lower add the yeast and mix together.
  10. Observe for bubbles. Once bubbling starts, add a lid to the pot and cover with a towel or old t-shirt.
  11. Wait 24 hours.
  12. You can now begin bottling the mead.
  13. Once bottled, add some raisins to the fluid with one teaspoon of sugar per litre.
  14. Store in your fridge for 3-5 days, release gas from the bottles in the morning and at night.

Notes

  1. This recipe is proportional, if you want a smaller batch, adjust each ingredient by the same fraction.
  2. The raisins are used for measuring the state of the mead. The more of them that float the closer it is to being done.
    • Note that it is possible that not all of them will float by the time the mead is finished.
  3. Try experimenting with adding fruits or heather.
  4. Adjust sugar / honey levels for sweetness / sourness, etc.
